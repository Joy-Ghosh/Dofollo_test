What Is An Internal Link?

Internal links are essentially bridges to another destination. 

The idea of contextual internal links is to help the audience of your website or blog to find more information about a topic, the page you are linking to should be a sub topic of your current page.

Hence, you should only hyperlink to pages that add more value to your topic and not just add internal links for the sake of it.

The bridge should take you to a different destination but that's related to your original location, it only retains relevance if the destination is related to the origin.

Internal Link Best Practices.

Choosing Your Target Page.

The First step is to choose the page you wish to rank for. This is probably the easiest as most of you probably already have the page in mind. But here are a few things you should keep in mind when showing the target page.

  • The Page should be relevant to the central theme of your website. If you do not have pages that are relevant to your target page, you will not have any pages to link from.
  • The keywords or phrases you are trying to rank for should be within means; do not go for keywords that are very hard to rank for.

Discover High Authority Page To Build Internal Links.

To rank higher, you need links from valuable pages. One way to find valuable pages is to find high-authority pages that are also semantically close to your target page.

You can use tools like Ahref and SEMRush to find pages with the most backlinks, but for the purpose of this demonstration, we would use the Google Search Console Tool as the tool of our choice to find pages with the most backlinks.

How To Find High Authority Pages on Google Search Console Tool?

On your Google Search Console, locate the “Links” option on the left-hand menu. This would show you both the internal link & external links. 

Under internal links, you will find the “Top linked pages externally” Click on the “more” option under the initial list. 

This would open the list of pages with their number of backlinks mentioned on the right.

You also have the option to export the report, from there you can find the pages that are relevant to your target page & sort them according to their number of backlinks if your donor list is sizable.

Build Internal Links on Pages With Organic Traffic

Another way to find a list of your prospective donor pages for your internal links is to find pages on your website that already have some organic traffic to it.

Pages with organic traffic are great indicators of value. If Google is ranking your page high enough to get organic clicks it is assigning the age some authority. 

You can not only distribute that page authority to semantically related pages on your website, but internal links on pages with organic traffic also divert real users to your target pages.

This flow of real visitors is a strong ranking signal for Google's search ranking algorithm.

What Are Semantic Relevance?

Saying two pieces of content share semantic relevance means these two pieces of content are closely related in terms of their topic coverage. 

For example, a blog post related to on-page SEO would be semantically related to an internal link-building blog post like this one.

Use Descriptive Anchor Text For Internal Links

While Selecting anchor text for your internal links, try to choose descriptive anchor text. Ideall,y your anchor texts should describe the content of your target page.

This linking style helps both search engines and your audience understand the context of your target page, which is great for your user experience.

Avoid building internal links with just one of two words as their anchor text.

Choose A Contrasting Hyperlink Text Color

Building internal links is not just for SEO ranking; it also helps your users to find valuable information on your website.

Make sure your internal link stands out from the regular text well enough so your audience can visually distinguish them while scanning the webpage, even on a mobile device.

Limit The Number Of Link In A Page

Internal links are great for improving the ranking of your pages, but they should be built in moderation.

You wouldn’t want to build too many of them as that would dilute their link juice, having too many links from a page would reduce the average influence of each internal link from that Page.

How Many Internal Links Is Too Many?

But how many is too many? Well, for internal links, most SEO experts recommend sticking below internal links per to words. But, most bigger well-known blogs usually build more internal links, especially on older blog posts.


If you are not sure what limit you should set for yourself, you can use Dofollo to check the behemoths, your competitors or the page ranking at the top of Google search and see how many internal links they are adding to their blogs.

Avoid No-follow Links

Conventional wisdom is, Do-follow links pass link juice as well as navigating the user while No-follow links do not pass the link authority, they are used for navigational purposes.

While, If No-Follow has zero link authority or lower link authority could be up for debate, it is best to avoid the no-follow when building internal links.

How to build dofollow links?

All links that you create through the WordPress editor are dofollow by default unless you add the nofollow tag.

Nofollow links have specified it by adding a “nofollow” tag.

Add External Links

Links help your pages to rank, but it's not just limited to backlinks and internal links. 

External links, too, can help you rank your pages. This might seem counterintuitive but a meaningful external link that elevates user experience does help you rank higher.

But what kind of external links should you build? Links to pages that would provide valuable information to your audience, relevance and usefulness are the key here.

How many external links should your page have?

Unless you have a listicle on your webpage, it is advisable to have only one or two external links.

A good practice is to make external links that open in new tabs instead of closing your own website and redirecting your audience there, as this would reduce your dwell time.

Also, external links should ideally not be at the beginning of the page.

What Are External Links?

External links are links on your page that divert your visitors to a different page outside your own website.
